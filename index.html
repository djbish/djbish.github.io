About

Ever Since I was young I have enjoyed making things. In 5th grade I started an invention "company" to help teachers with their problems. I made a "lead reuser" that held broken off pencil tips so that they could be used to write with.

This desire to make and solve problems lead me to pursue a degree in Mechanical Engineering at Columbia University. Since graduating I have worked as a Mechanical Engineer in various capacities. I have acquired a diverse set of skills essential to working on multi-disciplinary teams developing innovative technologies and products. My hands-on experience ranges from user research and brainstorming, prototyping and testing, to design for manfacture. I am always interested in a project that teaches me something new.
